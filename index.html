South Sudan Humanitarian Crisis

The world's newest nation is currently experiencing one of the worst humanitarian crises the world has ever seen. Drought, conflict, and economic distaster have further exacerbated the crisis and forced over 1.5 million people to seek refuge. Scroll down to learn more about the crisis in South Sudan.

The crisis unfolded

In 2011, after years of conflict surrounding the nation's oil wealth, South Sudan gained independence from it's norther neighbor, Sudan. Following the referendum for independence, it was thought that all violence and conflict in the nation would cease, however, not long after the vote, South Sudan found itself in the midst of a brutal civil war. A currupt president, widespread drought and subsequent famine, economic woes, and continuous violence has turned South Sudan into a humanitarian disaster. Many organizations are working to try and improve the situation in South Sudan, including the World Food Programme, UNCHR, USAID, and so on. This site is designed to be an overview of the many factors that are contributing to this humanitarian crisis.
